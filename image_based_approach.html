Shape To Music

Image-Based Approach by Sofia Cassara

The Image-Based approach creates music by dividing the image into sub-images, then calculating the ratio of black pixels to total pixels (termed "density") for each sub-image. As the density increases, the notes get deeper; a density of 0 will be the highest note possible (C7) while a density of 100 will be the lowest note possible (C3). Different sub-image sizes yield different songs.

There are two kinds of topological images on this page. Cellular metamaterials, denoted 'CMM', feature a periodic pattern across the image. The other spinodoids, denoted 'Image', feature non-periodic patterns generated through research. The material of both is a Titanium-Aluminum alloy.

Listen to a song by selecting an image, then selecting a sub-image size!
